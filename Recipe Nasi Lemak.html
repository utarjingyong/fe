Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a traditional Malay dish that holds the title of Malaysia's national dish. It is a flavorful combination of rice cooked in coconut milk, served with spicy sambal, crispy fried anchovies, roasted peanuts, boiled eggs, and fresh cucumber slices. Often paired with rendang or fried chicken, this dish is beloved for its rich and creamy flavors.

Watch the Video Tutorial

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 pandan leaf, knotted
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Boiled eggs
  • Fried crispy anchovies
  • Roasted peanuts
  • Spicy sambal (chili paste)
  • Cucumber slices

Procedure

  1. Wash the rice until the water runs clear. Then, combine the rice, coconut milk, water, pandan leaf, and salt in a rice cooker. Cook the rice until it's done.
  2. Prepare the sambal by blending onions, garlic, chilies, and belacan (shrimp paste). Fry the paste until fragrant, then add tamarind juice, sugar, and salt. Simmer until the sambal thickens.
  3. Fry the anchovies until they are crispy, and roast the peanuts in a dry pan until they are golden brown.
  4. To serve, place a portion of rice on a plate. Add a boiled egg, fried anchovies, roasted peanuts, cucumber slices, and a generous spoonful of sambal.
  5. Enjoy your homemade nasi lemak!
