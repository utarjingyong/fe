Butternut Squash Curry

Butternut Squash Curry is a rich and flavorful dish featuring tender chunks of butternut squash simmered in a creamy, spiced curry sauce. The curry combines the natural sweetness of the squash with aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, and turmeric, along with a hint of heat from chili. Often enriched with coconut milk, this dish offers a comforting, satisfying meal that pairs perfectly with rice or naan.

How to Make Butternut Squash Curry

Ingredients

  • 1 large butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into cubes
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup spinach leaves (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish (optional)

Procedure

  1. Dice the onion and carrots. Chop the butternut squash into cubes (or use pre-chopped butternut squash).
  2. Mince the garlic, mince or grate the ginger and lemongrass, and thinly slice the Thai chile peppers (if using).
  3. Sauté the diced onions and carrots in a bit of coconut oil in a deep sauté pan or Dutch oven until lightly browned.
  4. Add the garlic, ginger, lemongrass, chile peppers, and curry paste, and stir frequently for 2 minutes.
  5. Deglaze the pan with vegetable broth, then add in the coconut milk, butternut squash, coconut sugar, and soy sauce.
  6. Simmer the curry for 20 minutes, until the squash is tender.
  7. Blend about half of the curry using an immersion blender. If using a stand blender, transfer half of the curry, blend, then return it to the pan.
  8. Add the chickpeas to the pan and bring to a simmer. Then add in the baby spinach or kale and cook until wilted.
  9. Finally, stir in the Thai basil (if using) and/or chopped cilantro.

Prep Time

15 minutes

Cook Time

35 minutes

Serves

4 people
