Rendang

Rendang is a rich and flavorful slow-cooked dry curry originating from Indonesia, but popular across Malaysia. It is made with beef (or sometimes chicken), slowly simmered in coconut milk and a blend of aromatic spices including ginger, turmeric, lemongrass, and chilies. The result is tender meat with deep, intense flavors.

Watch the Video Tutorial

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 2 lemongrass stalks, bruised
  • 5 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 turmeric leaves, shredded (optional)
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Spice paste: Shallots, garlic, ginger, galangal, chilies, and turmeric

Procedure

  1. Blend the shallots, garlic, ginger, galangal, chilies, and turmeric into a smooth paste. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat some oil and sauté the spice paste until fragrant. Add the beef cubes and cook until the beef is browned.
  3. Add the coconut milk, lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, turmeric leaves, and tamarind paste. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
  4. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for 2-3 hours, stirring occasionally, until the meat is tender and the sauce is thick and reduced.
  5. Season with salt to taste and serve the rendang with steamed rice or ketupat (rice cakes).
