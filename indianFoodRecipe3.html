Masala Chai

Masala Chai, often simply called "chai," is a flavorful and aromatic Indian tea that blends black tea with a mixture of spices and herbs. The term "masala" means "spice mix" in Hindi, and the blend typically includes ingredients such as cardamom, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and black pepper. To make Masala Chai, spices are usually simmered in water to release their flavors, and then black tea leaves are added. The mixture is boiled with milk and sweetened with sugar or honey to create a rich, warming beverage. It's commonly enjoyed in India and around the world for its comforting, spicy taste and invigorating properties.

How to Make the Perfect Masala Chai

Ingredients

  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons loose black tea leaves
  • 2-3 tablespoons sugar (to taste)
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
  • 1-2 cloves
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, sliced
  • Optional: black peppercorns or star anise for extra flavor

Procedure

  1. In a medium saucepan, combine water, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, and ginger. Bring to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat and let the spices simmer for about 5 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  3. Add the tea leaves and continue to simmer for another 2-3 minutes, depending on how strong you like your tea.
  4. Stir in the milk and sugar, and bring the mixture back to a gentle boil.
  5. Once boiling, remove from heat and strain the tea into cups using a fine-mesh strainer or cheesecloth.
  6. Garnish with a sprinkle of ground cardamom or a cinnamon stick if desired, and serve hot.

Prep Time

10 minutes

Cook Time

10 minutes

Serves

2 cups
