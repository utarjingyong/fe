Kung Pao Chicken

Kung Pao Chicken is a popular Chinese dish renowned for its flavorful combination of tender chicken, crunchy peanuts, and vibrant vegetables. This spicy stir-fry features chicken cooked with a tangy, slightly sweet sauce made from soy sauce, vinegar, and hoisin sauce, and is enhanced with the bold heat of dried red chilies and Sichuan peppercorns. Traditionally served with steamed rice, Kung Pao Chicken is celebrated for its rich, spicy flavor and delightful texture, making it a beloved choice in Chinese cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450 g) boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp hoisin sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/2 cup unsalted dry roasted peanuts
  • 1/2 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup diced green bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup sliced green onions
  • 3-4 dried red chilies (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp Sichuan peppercorns
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced

Procedure

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and cornstarch. This will be your marinade and sauce mixture. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 15 minutes.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add the dried red chilies and Sichuan peppercorns (if using) and stir-fry for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the minced garlic and ginger to the skillet, and stir-fry for an additional 30 seconds until aromatic.
  4. Add the marinated chicken to the skillet and cook for about 5-7 minutes, stirring frequently, until the chicken is browned and cooked through.
  5. Add the diced red and green bell peppers, onion, and peanuts to the skillet. Stir-fry for an additional 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  6. Pour the reserved sauce mixture over the chicken and vegetables. Stir well to coat everything evenly and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the sauce has thickened and is well incorporated.
  7. Garnish with sliced green onions and serve the Kung Pao Chicken hot with steamed rice.
  8. Enjoy your flavorful Kung Pao Chicken!
